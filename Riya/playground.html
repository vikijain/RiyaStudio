The quiet miracle of vibe coding

You don’t need a CS degree to build something that feels real. You need curiosity, clear instructions, taste for quality, and the discipline to keep asking AI “make it smoother / fix that bug / make it more addictive.”

Right way: you own the vision, test like a user, refuse half-broken demos. Wrong way: paste once and hope. Wonders come from the loop — describe → try → refine → ship.

01 · Intent

Describe the wonder

“Build a 2048 game with high scores and smooth tiles” beats “write some JS.” Clarity is the superpower.

02 · Iterate

Play it yourself

If it’s not fun for half an hour, ask AI why. Polish, difficulty, feedback — that’s product sense, not syntax.

03 · Ship

Put it on the internet

A private demo is practice. A public arcade is proof — intentional tech practice, shipped in the open.
