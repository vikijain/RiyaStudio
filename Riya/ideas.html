// 04 — Food for Thought

Ideas I'm not sure about yet

Not pitches, not plans — just things I keep thinking about. Some are probably impossible. Some might already exist. I'm writing them down anyway, unfinished, in case that's useful to someone (including future me).

SPECULATIVE JULY 2026

A household robot that actually replaces domestic help

Every "home robot" I've seen solves one narrow task — vacuuming, or folding, or reminding you about groceries. None of them do what a maid actually does: read a messy, unstructured home, notice what needs doing without being told, and adapt every single day. I don't know if that's a hardware problem, a perception problem, or a cost problem — probably all three. I'm not sure it's even a good idea socially (a lot of livelihoods depend on this work). But I keep wondering what the smallest believable version of it would look like.

What's the smallest "chore" a robot would need to solve, end-to-end, unsupervised, before this stops sounding like science fiction?

EARLY THOUGHT JULY 2026

Credit scoring that explains itself in plain language

Most credit decisions I've made in 12 years come down to a rating methodology that the person being rated never really sees or understands. What if every credit decision came with a short, honest, human-readable explanation — not a compliance disclosure, an actual explanation — even when the answer is no?

JUST A NOTE JULY 2026

A "second passport" for skills, not travel

My travel page tracks every country I've stamped. I don't have anything similar for skills — a record of every genuinely hard thing I've learned to do, with the messy attempts included, not just the polished résumé version. Might build this as a page here eventually.

More of these get added as they show up. If one of them sparks something for you, I'd genuinely like to hear it.
